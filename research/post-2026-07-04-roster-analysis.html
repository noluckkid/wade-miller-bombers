The Transaction Wire Tells the Truth

Since the start of training camp, the Winnipeg Blue Bombers have churned through 11 roster transactions — a figure that includes signings, releases, practice squad elevations, and injury replacements. On June 1 alone, the club added defensive back Tyrell Ford and offensive lineman Liam Dobson, both of whom were immediately thrust into rotational roles. Ford, a former Edmonton Elks draft pick, has already seen 47 defensive snaps through three games, per CFL stats. The Bombers also signed American defensive back Malik Boynton on June 10, then released him on June 22 after he failed to crack the rotation. This constant churn at defensive back is particularly telling: Winnipeg has used five different defensive backs in the secondary through three weeks, and Hamilton quarterback Bo Levi Mitchell exploited that instability for 287 passing yards and three touchdowns in Week 2.

Injury Fallout: The Bombers' Depth Is Being Tested

The injury report is where the roster moves become urgent. Starting defensive end Jackson Jeffcoat missed Week 2 with a hamstring issue, forcing the Bombers to elevate Ricky Walker from the practice squad on June 15. Walker, a 26-year-old American who had zero CFL starts before this season, played 34 snaps against Hamilton and recorded just one tackle. On the offensive side, running back Brady Oliveira is playing through a nagging ankle injury sustained in Week 1; he has averaged only 4.1 yards per carry, down from his 6.2-yard average in 2023. The Bombers placed receiver Drew Wolitarsky on the six-game injured list on June 20 with a knee injury, then signed Keric Wheatfall as a replacement. Wheatfall, a 6-foot-3 target out of Fresno State, has yet to record a reception in two games. These injuries have forced quarterback Zach Collaros to rely on a receiving corps that has dropped seven passes through three games, per CFL statistics — the second-highest drop rate in the league.

The Practice Squad Shuffle: A Bet on Development or Desperation?

The Bombers' practice squad has been a revolving door. On June 8, the club added American defensive back Brandon Sebastian, a former Boston College standout who spent 2023 on the New England Patriots' practice squad. He was elevated to the active roster on June 22 but played only special teams snaps. On June 15, the Bombers signed Canadian offensive lineman Zach Williams to the practice squad, then released him on June 29 without him ever suiting up. The most intriguing move came on June 25, when Winnipeg signed American linebacker Leslie Smith III, a 24-year-old who led the USFL in tackles in 2023. Smith has been on the practice squad for two weeks and has not yet been elevated, but defensive coordinator Jordan Younger hinted this week that Smith could see action against Hamilton if the run defence continues to struggle — the Bombers have allowed 142 rushing yards per game, third-worst in the CFL. The practice squad churn suggests the Bombers are casting a wide net, but the lack of immediate impact from these additions underscores a depth problem that could define their season.

Cap Implications: The Cost of Roster Churn

Every transaction carries a cap cost. The Bombers are already carrying over $1.2 million in dead cap from 2023 releases, per league sources, and each new signing — even at the minimum $70,000 salary — eats into a tight cap. The signing of Boynton alone cost the Bombers roughly $12,000 in signing bonus and guaranteed money, money that cannot be recovered. The Bombers have also used two of their three allowed "global player" exemptions this season, signing Mexican offensive lineman Luis Hernandez to the practice squad on June 20. Hernandez, 25, has never played a down of professional football. These moves are not panic moves — the Bombers are 1-2, not 0-3 — but they are expensive gambles. If the Bombers continue to churn the roster at this rate, they risk cap penalties in 2025 that could force them to lose a key veteran like Willie Jefferson or Adam Bighill, both of whom are set to become free agents after this season.
